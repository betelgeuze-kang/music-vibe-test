# E1 Editorial Content Integrity

E1 aligns the music content with the editorial BD1 interface. It does not add new songs; it improves how the existing catalog is named, scored, explained, and presented.

## Human-edited archetypes

Korean result titles are listening scenes rather than transliterations or personality-test characters:

| Stable ID | Korean display title | English title |
|---|---|---|
| `midnight-dreamer` | 밤의 잔향 | Midnight Dreamer |
| `neon-runner` | 네온 주행 | Neon Runner |
| `warm-vinyl` | 따뜻한 레코드 | Warm Vinyl |
| `cosmic-architect` | 소리 설계자 | Cosmic Architect |
| `quiet-cinematic` | 조용한 장면 | Quiet Cinematic |
| `rhythm-connector` | 함께 타는 리듬 | Rhythm Connector |
| `electric-explorer` | 전류를 따라 | Electric Explorer |
| `golden-chorus` | 함께 부르는 후렴 | Golden Chorus |

The IDs, shared-profile tokens, and legacy MBTI mappings remain stable.

## Sixty-track editorial set

`src/v2/data/editorial-tracks.mjs` contains sixty existing catalog tracks selected across the eight archetypes, six listening contexts, and bridge-playlist use cases. Every edited track includes:

- six manually assigned taste dimensions,
- vocality, danceability, and pace,
- at least two listening scenes,
- unique Korean and English liner notes,
- Spotify, YouTube, and Apple Music search destinations.

Edited data replaces the archetype-centroid fallback for those tracks. The remaining catalog stays available through the deterministic fallback until a later free-use catalog pipeline is introduced.

## Recommendation behavior

The recommendation and match domains:

1. use manually edited track profiles when available,
2. apply a small editorial priority without removing relevance or diversity,
3. prefer the track’s liner note as the explanation,
4. add dimensional contrast only for adjacent and exploration selections,
5. preserve the three-core / one-adjacent / one-exploration plan.

CI evaluates the complete archetype-by-context matrix. Every five-track list must contain at least two edited anchors, the matrix must average at least three edited tracks per list, and at least 75% of lists must contain three or more.

## Fixed home showcase

The home showcase is no longer generated by the recommendation engine. Nine tracks are fixed in `src/v2/data/home-showcase.mjs`:

- three tracks explaining the sample profile,
- three tracks for a night-walk example,
- three tracks bridging two sample tastes.

This keeps the public storefront stable when recommendation weights or fallback catalog data change.

## Softer score presentation

Internal calculations retain exact `0..100` scores. User-facing presentation emphasizes bands:

```text
매우 낮은 편
낮은 편
균형
높은 편
매우 높은 편
```

Visible numbers are rounded to ten-point steps where they are useful. Match screens likewise lead with labels for Resonance and Discovery while retaining the exact internal values for ranking and analytics.

## Deferred catalog expansion

E1 deliberately does not add songs. A future pipeline may collect music that is legally usable at no cost, but ingestion must capture provenance, license, creator, source URL, permitted use, attribution requirements, audio normalization, and review status before publication.
